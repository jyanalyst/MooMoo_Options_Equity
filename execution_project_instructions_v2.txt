## POSITION SIZING FORMULA (UNIFIED)

**Problem:** Fixed dollar limits ignore both individual stock volatility AND market regime volatility.

**Solution:** Single unified formula incorporating both factors.

### FORMULA:
```
Position Size = (Capital × Base%) × VIX Multiplier / Volatility Factor

Where:
- Base% = 15% (baseline allocation)
- VIX Multiplier = Regime-based scaling (see table below)
- Volatility Factor = IV Rank / 50 (normalizes individual stock risk)
```

### VIX MULTIPLIER TABLE:
- VIX <14: 0× (don't trade)
- VIX 14-18: 0.5×
- VIX 18-25: 1.0×
- VIX >25: 1.5×

### VOLATILITY FACTOR CALCULATION:
- Low vol stock (IV Rank 30): Factor = 0.6 → Increase size by 67%
- Medium vol stock (IV Rank 50): Factor = 1.0 → Standard size
- High vol stock (IV Rank 80): Factor = 1.6 → Reduce size by 38%

### PRACTICAL EXAMPLES ($50,000 capital):

**VIX = 22 (Normal Regime), IV Rank = 50 (Medium Vol):**
- Position Size = $50K × 15% × 1.0 / 1.0 = **$7,500**

**VIX = 28 (Aggressive Regime), IV Rank = 65 (Higher Vol):**
- Position Size = $50K × 15% × 1.5 / 1.3 = **$8,654**

**VIX = 16 (Cautious Regime), IV Rank = 70 (High Vol):**
- Position Size = $50K × 15% × 0.5 / 1.4 = **$2,679**

**VIX = 30 (Aggressive Regime), IV Rank = 40 (Low Vol - rare scenario):**
- Position Size = $50K × 15% × 1.5 / 0.8 = **$14,063**

**The formula automatically:**
- Scales UP total deployment when VIX is high (premium rich)
- Scales DOWN individual positions when stock IV Rank is high (idiosyncratic risk)
- Prevents trading when VIX <14 (insufficient edge)

## UNIVERSAL RULES (ALL STRATEGIES)

### 1. EARNINGS MANAGEMENT
**Never ENTER within 14 days of earnings.**

**If already in position with <7 days to earnings:**
- **Profitable position:** Close early, lock gains
- **Underwater + high conviction:** Roll to post-earnings expiry (only if stock thesis intact)
- **Post-earnings IV crush opportunity:** Sell NEW premium after announcement (restart Wheel)

### 2. STOP LOSS PROTOCOL (MECHANICAL - NO DISCRETION)

**Tier 1 - Primary Stop (Always Applied):**
- Exit when position loss = **200% of premium received**
- Example: Sold put for $2.00 → Exit at -$4.00 loss
- This is a HARD STOP - no exceptions, no waiting

**Tier 2 - Early Exit Triggers (Risk Management):**
Apply these BEFORE hitting 200% stop:
- If loss >150% premium AND <10 DTE → Close immediately (assignment risk too high)
- If loss >150% premium AND stock breaches strike → Close immediately (avoid assignment at bad price)
- If loss >175% premium AND unfavorable news → Close immediately (don't wait for final $0.50)

**Tier 3 - Assignment Decision (If stop triggered at expiration):**
- **If stock thesis still intact:** Accept assignment, begin covered call phase at cost basis + 5-10%
- **If thesis broken:** Take the loss, exit position, free capital for better opportunities
- **Thesis broken indicators:** Fraud accusations, bankruptcy risk, sector collapse, deteriorating fundamentals

**Tier 4 - Catastrophic Stop (Black Swan):**
- Exit if loss reaches **50% of collateral at risk**
- Only triggers in extreme scenarios (company-specific disaster)
- Example: Sold $100 strike put → exit if loss exceeds $50

**Key principle:** Your stop loss should trigger BEFORE assignment in most cases. Assignment should be a deliberate choice for quality stocks, not a forced outcome from hitting stop loss.

### 3. LIQUIDITY FILTERS (MANDATORY)
**Minimum standards for ALL trades:**
- Open Interest: >100 contracts (ensures exit liquidity)
- Bid-Ask Spread: <10% of mid price (prevents slippage erosion)

**Example:**
- Mid price = $2.00
- Bid = $1.85, Ask = $2.15
- Spread = $0.30 = 15% of mid → **REJECT** (too wide)

**Why this matters:** Wide spreads can cost you 10-15% of profit on entry AND exit. Over 50 trades, poor liquidity destroys 20-30% of total edge.

### 4. GREEKS TARGETS
**For cash-secured puts (Wheel entry):**
- **Delta:** 0.20-0.30 (70-80% probability of expiring worthless)
- **Theta:** Minimum $0.10/day per contract (daily decay must justify risk)
- **Vega:** Net short vega (you profit when IV decreases)

**Check Greeks BEFORE entry, not after.**

**Why these ranges:**
- Delta 0.20-0.30 = optimal balance between premium and assignment risk
- Theta $0.10+/day = ensures time decay edge is meaningful
- Short vega = profits from mean reversion (IV compression after spikes)

### 5. SECTOR DIVERSITY & CORRELATION
**To prevent concentration risk:**
- **Max 2 positions per sector** (Tech, Financials, Healthcare, Consumer, etc.)
- **Verify correlation <0.6** between new position and existing positions
- **During VIX >25 regimes:** Can have 3 positions in one sector IF they're top-tier quality (e.g., MSFT + GOOGL + AAPL allowed)

**How to check correlation:**
Use 30-day price correlation. If you're adding GOOGL and already hold MSFT:
- MSFT/GOOGL correlation ≈0.75 → Acceptable if only 2 tech positions
- If you also hold NVDA → correlation too high, skip GOOGL

**Why this matters:** During sector rotations (e.g., tech selloff March 2025), correlated positions hit stops simultaneously, creating cascade losses.

## ASSIGNMENT READINESS & TRIAGE

**"Assignment = opportunity" is only true if you still want to own the stock at assignment.**

### BEFORE Selling CSP (Assignment Prevention):
1. **Cash flow check:** Do I have 50% of strike value in liquid cash? (Not margin, actual cash)
2. **Thesis strength:** Would I be happy owning this stock for 6-12 months if assigned tomorrow?
3. **Covered call planning:** What strike would I sell calls at if assigned? Is there reasonable premium available?
4. **Multi-assignment scenario:** If assigned on all current CSPs simultaneously, can I handle the capital lock-up?

**If any answer is "no" → Don't sell the put. This isn't a good Wheel candidate.**

### AT ASSIGNMENT (Immediate Triage):

**Step 1 - Re-verify thesis at current price:**
- Has anything fundamental changed since I sold the put?
- Is the stock still in my quality universe?
- Would I buy this stock today at assignment price?

**Step 2 - Decision tree:**

**If thesis INTACT:**
- Accept assignment (this is Phase 2 of Wheel)
- Immediately sell covered call for next monthly expiration
- Strike = cost basis + 5-10% (or first strike with >20 delta)
- Update capital calculations (this capital is now locked)
- Journal as new position (covered call is separate trade from CSP)

**If thesis BROKEN (fraud, bankruptcy, sector collapse, deteriorating fundamentals):**
- Exit immediately at market open
- Do NOT sell covered call to "lower cost basis" - this compounds the mistake
- Take the loss, free capital for better opportunities
- Journal the thesis breakdown - what did you miss?

**If thesis UNCERTAIN (stock down 20%+ but fundamentals unclear):**
- Sell VERY short-dated covered call (7-14 DTE) at cost basis or slightly lower
- Use this time to research and decide: Keep or exit?
- If next earnings approaching: Exit before earnings rather than hold through uncertainty
- Maximum 2 uncertain positions allowed - exit others to preserve capital

### MULTI-ASSIGNMENT CRISIS PROTOCOL:

**If assigned on 3+ positions in same week (VIX >30 scenarios):**

1. **Immediate prioritization:** Rank stocks by conviction (1 = strongest, 5 = weakest)
2. **Capital triage:**
   - Top 2 convictions: Sell covered calls at cost basis + 5-10%
   - Middle tier: Sell 14 DTE calls at cost basis (break-even acceptable)
   - Weakest conviction: Exit immediately if thesis questionable
3. **Deployment halt:** No new CSPs until at least 2 positions exit via calls
4. **Cash flow check:** Ensure you can handle margin requirements if further assigned

**Key principle:** Multiple assignments is only acceptable if you truly wanted to own all these stocks. If you're uncomfortable, you over-deployed in Phase 1.

## WIN RATE & EDGE TRACKING

**Why track:** Without data, you can't distinguish luck from edge. Your strategy has ~70% theoretical win rate - you need 30+ trades to verify edge is real.

**Monthly metrics to calculate:**

### 1. Win Rate
**Formula:** (Winning trades / Total trades) × 100
- **Target:** >65% for CSPs
- **Action if <60%:** Process review required
- **Context:** 10 trades at 50% win rate is normal variance, not edge erosion

### 2. Average Premium Capture
**Formula:** (Actual premium received / Mid price at entry) × 100
- **Target:** >95% (slippage <5%)
- **Action if <90%:** Review entry execution - are you being too aggressive with limit orders?
- **Context:** Slippage compounds - 10% slippage on 50 trades costs you 15-20% of total profit

### 3. IV Timing Score
**Formula:** Average (IV Rank at entry - IV Rank at exit)
- **Target:** +10 to +20 percentile points
- **Positive = Correct:** Selling when IV high, closing when IV low (mean reversion captured)
- **Negative = Wrong:** Selling when IV low, closing when IV high (you're doing the opposite of your edge)
- **Action if negative:** You're entering in wrong VIX regimes or holding too long

### 4. Theta Capture Rate
**Formula:** (Actual P&L from theta / Projected theta at entry) × 100
- **Target:** >80% of projected theta
- **Calculation:** If trade held 10 days with $0.15/day theta → expected $1.50, did you capture >$1.20?
- **Action if <70%:** You're closing too early (not letting theta work) or volatility moves are overwhelming decay

### 5. VIX Regime Performance
**Analysis:** Break down returns by VIX regime at entry
- **Expected pattern:** VIX >25 trades should have highest premium but highest variance
- **Red flag:** If VIX <14 trades are profitable, you're getting lucky, not demonstrating edge
- **Action:** Confirm you're actually generating best risk-adjusted returns during VIX 18-25 regimes

### 6. Stop Loss Discipline
**Metric:** (Trades hitting stop / Total losses) × 100
- **Target:** >80% (most losses should trigger stop, not drift to max loss)
- **Red flag if <50%:** You're overriding stops or not setting them properly
- **Context:** Stop discipline prevents catastrophic losses

### 7. Early Close Rate
**Metric:** (Trades closed at 50% profit / Total wins) × 100
- **Target:** >60% (most wins should close at 50% profit target)
- **Red flag if <40%:** You're holding for max profit and giving back gains
- **Context:** Early closes double your win rate by freeing capital for new trades

**Add all 7 metrics to monthly review template. Track trends over time (rolling 3-month average).**
